Soft Duplicate Detection:

Brief: For search-based motion planning in continuous space, there are no states that are exactly the same. To this end, we exploit soft duplicate detection to help prioritize the expansion of states, thus trying to avoid wasting resources expanding states that do little contribution to the search.

Wei Du, Sungkyun Kim, Oren Salzman and Maxim Likhachev, "Escaping Local Minima in Search-Based Planning using Soft Duplicate Detection", Proceedings of the IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS), 2019

Multi-Resolution A*:

Brief: Considering search-based motion planning on large scale or high dimensional domain. Planning in highly discretized space requires a lot of computation resources whereas in low resolution sacrifices the completeness of algorithms. So why not plan with multiple resolutions simultaneously?
